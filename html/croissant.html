Croissant

This croissant is a masterpiece of patience and precision — 27 delicate layers of buttery dough, folded and baked to golden perfection. Each bite is crisp and flaky on the outside, soft and airy on the inside, with a rich aroma that only real butter can bring. It’s the kind of croissant that makes mornings feel special and every bite worth savoring.

  • Preparation time:
    30 minutes
  • Cook time:
    1 hours
  • Resting time:
    8 hours
  • Baking time:
    30 minutes
  • Total time:
    10 hours
  • Total portion:
    12 croissants

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast
  • 1 1/4 cups whole milk, warm
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold
  • 1 egg, for egg wash

Directions

  • In a large bowl, combine flour, sugar, yeast, and salt. Gradually add warm milk and mix until a dough forms. Knead for about 5-7 minutes until smooth. Cover and let it rise for 1 hour.
  • Roll out the dough into a rectangle. Place cold butter between two sheets of parchment paper and pound it with a rolling pin to flatten it. Place the butter onto one half of the dough and fold the other half over it. Seal the edges.
  • Roll out the dough again into a rectangle. Fold it into thirds like a letter. Wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate for 30 minutes. Repeat this rolling and folding process two more times, refrigerating for 30 minutes each time.
  • After the final fold, roll out the dough into a large rectangle. Cut into triangles. Roll each triangle from the base to the tip to form croissant shapes. Place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  • Cover and let them rise for another 1-2 hours until puffy. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Brush the croissants with beaten egg for a shiny finish.
  • Bake for 15-20 minutes until golden brown. Let them cool slightly before serving. Enjoy your homemade croissants!
